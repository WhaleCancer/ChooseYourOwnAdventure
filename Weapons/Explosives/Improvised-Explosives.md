# Improvised Explosives

**Various Sources**  
**Type:** Homemade / improvised explosive devices

Improvised explosive devices (IEDs) made from readily available materials. In 1985 British Columbia, improvised explosives could be constructed from fertilizer, household chemicals, or stolen commercial explosives. Encountered with criminals, terrorists, or individuals with knowledge of explosives. The construction of improvised explosives indicates serious criminal intent, terrorist activity, or highly dangerous individuals. More common in rural areas where materials might be more readily available, or in contexts involving organized crime or extremist groups. The presence of improvised explosives suggests a high level of threat and criminal sophistication.
