# C1A1 Self-Loading Rifle

**Manufacturer:** Canadian Arsenals Limited  
**Caliber:** 7.62×51mm NATO  
**Type:** Semi-automatic battle rifle

The standard service rifle for the Canadian Armed Forces from the early 1950s until 1984, when it began being replaced by the C7. A Canadian-manufactured variant of the Belgian FN FAL, chambered in 7.62×51mm NATO with a 20-round detachable box magazine. Known for its reliability, accuracy, and powerful cartridge. In 1985 British Columbia, still widely in use with Canadian military units as the transition to C7 was ongoing. Also used by some RCMP tactical units for specialized operations. Occasionally encountered in criminal hands through theft or illegal acquisition from military sources. The C1A1 represents the old guard of Canadian military small arms.
