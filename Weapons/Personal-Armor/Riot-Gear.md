# Riot Gear

**Manufacturer:** Various  
**Type:** Protective equipment for crowd control

Protective equipment used by police for riot control and crowd management. In 1985 British Columbia, riot gear would be encountered with police forces during protests, riots, or large-scale crowd control situations. Includes riot shields (transparent or opaque), riot helmets with face protection, padded body protection, and sometimes leg and arm guards. Encountered with police tactical units, riot squads, or officers deployed for crowd control. Riot gear provides protection against thrown objects, melee weapons, and impacts while allowing officers to maintain crowd control capabilities. The presence of riot gear indicates police preparation for violent crowd situations or civil unrest.
