# Improvised Armor

**Various Sources**  
**Type:** Homemade / improvised protective equipment

Protective equipment improvised from available materials. In 1985 British Columbia, improvised armor could include layers of clothing, padding, or makeshift protective gear constructed by criminals or individuals seeking protection. Can include thick clothing, leather jackets, or other materials used to provide some protection against impacts or, to a limited extent, weapons. Encountered with criminals who lack access to proper body armor, survivalists, or individuals in unusual circumstances. Improvised armor provides minimal protection compared to proper body armor but may offer some defense against melee weapons or low-velocity projectiles. The presence of improvised armor indicates individuals seeking protection without access to proper equipment, often associated with criminal activity or survivalist scenarios.
