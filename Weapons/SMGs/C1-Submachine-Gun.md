**Type:** Submachine gun

Canada's licensed version of the British Sterling SMG, adopted in 1958 and remaining in service until 1988. Manufactured by Canadian Arsenals Limited with a simplified design featuring a single return spring and non-helically grooved bolt. Chambered in 9mm with a 30-round magazine and a cyclic rate of approximately 550 rounds per minute. In 1985 British Columbia, encountered primarily with Canadian military personnel, especially armored vehicle crews, special operations units, or personnel requiring a compact automatic weapon. The C1 was being phased out but still widely in service. Not available to civilians, but may be encountered in military operations or, rarely, stolen and in criminal hands.
