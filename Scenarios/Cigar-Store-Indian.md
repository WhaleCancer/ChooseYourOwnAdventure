# Adventure: Cigar Store Indian

**Type:** Scenario / Adventure hook  
**Location:** Victoria, British Columbia  
**Era:** 1985

## Overview

In Victoria, a cigar store Indian statue comes to life, possessed by a First Nations ghost. This creates a supernatural investigation scenario involving Indigenous spirits, possession, and the intersection of colonial history with the paranormal.

## The Statue

- A traditional cigar store Indian statue (a problematic colonial artifact)
- Located in Victoria, possibly in a shop, museum, or public space
- Suddenly becomes animated and possessed

## The Possession

- The statue is possessed by a First Nations ghost
- The spirit may be seeking justice, revenge, or simply trying to communicate
- The possession may be triggered by:
  - A thin veil event
  - Desecration of sacred ground
  - Contact with other supernatural forces
  - A specific ritual or ceremony

## Investigation Hooks

- Reports of a "living statue" causing disturbances
- Witnesses claim the statue moves, speaks, or acts on its own
- Connection to local First Nations history or sacred sites
- May be related to other supernatural events in the area
- Could involve [[Factions/Circle-of-Watchers|Circle of Watchers]] or other Indigenous groups

## Potential Complications

- The statue may be dangerous or protective
- The spirit may have specific goals or demands
- Colonial history and cultural sensitivity issues
- May attract unwanted attention from various factions
- Could be a trap or manipulation by hostile forces

## Resolution

- The spirit may need to be laid to rest
- The statue may need to be returned to proper hands
- Historical wrongs may need to be addressed
- The possession may be a symptom of a larger problem
- Could lead to deeper investigation of local supernatural activity
