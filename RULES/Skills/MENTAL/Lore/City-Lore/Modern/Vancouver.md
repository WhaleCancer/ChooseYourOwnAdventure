# Vancouver (1985)

**Settlement Type:** City  
**Population:** ~430,000 (1985)  
**Origin:** Founded as a port city in the late 19th century, Vancouver grew as a trading hub for the Pacific Rim and resource extraction (logging, mining).  
**Reason for Placement:** Natural deep-water harbor, proximity to Fraser River and rich hinterland, railway terminus connecting to eastern Canada.  
**Current Occupation:** Mixed population of European-Canadian, Chinese-Canadian, and other ethnic communities. Major port city and commercial center.

## Location Types

### City Gates / Highway Entrances (CG) - 2
- **Lion's Gate Bridge** - Northern entrance from North Vancouver
- **Highway 1/99 Interchange** - Eastern entrance from Fraser Valley
- **Pacific Border Crossing** - Southern entrance from United States (Peace Arch)
- **Sea Island / Airport Approach** - Western entrance via bridges to airport and Richmond

### Streets (ST) - 2
- **Downtown Core** - Central business district with office towers, shops, restaurants
- **Commercial Drive** - Italian and Portuguese neighborhood with cafes and shops
- **Main Street** - Mixed residential and commercial, working-class area
- **Granville Street** - Entertainment strip with bars, clubs, theaters

### Affluent Neighborhoods (WS) - 2
- **Shaughnessy** - Historic mansions, tree-lined streets, old money
- **West Vancouver** - Waterfront estates, expensive real estate
- **Kerrisdale** - Upper-middle-class residential area
- **Point Grey** - University area, expensive homes near UBC

### Poor Neighborhoods / Low-Income Areas (SA) - 3
- **Downtown Eastside** - Poverty, addiction, cheap hotels, SROs (Single Room Occupancy)
- **East Hastings** - Skid row, social services, crime, drug trade
- **Chinatown (east side)** - Aging buildings, some poverty, mixed with traditional Chinese businesses
- **Strathcona** - Working-class neighborhood, some rundown areas
- **Kitsilano (southern edge)** - Some low-income housing mixed with middle-class

### Docks / Port (DO) - 1
- **Vancouver Harbour** - Major container port, cruise ships, ferry terminals
- **Coal Harbour** - Industrial shipping, seaplanes
- **False Creek** - Smaller boats, marinas, Expo 86 preparations

### Marketplace / Commercial Districts (MP) - 2
- **Pacific Centre** - Major downtown shopping mall
- **Robson Street** - Shopping district, cafes, boutiques
- **Chinatown Market** - Traditional Chinese markets, groceries, restaurants
- **Gastown** - Historic area with shops, restaurants, nightlife

### Hotels (IN) - 1-2
- **Downtown Hotels** - Bayshore, Hyatt, Four Seasons, various mid-range hotels
- **Airport Hotels** - Near Vancouver International Airport
- **Budget Motels** - Along Kingsway, Main Street, and outskirts

### Bars / Pubs (TA) - 1-2
- **Gastown Pubs** - Historic pubs and bars
- **Granville Strip** - Bars, clubs, live music venues
- **Neighborhood Pubs** - Throughout residential areas

### Dive Bars / Seedy Bars (DD) - 1
- **Downtown Eastside Bars** - Rough bars, cheap drinks, dangerous clientele
- **Industrial Area Bars** - Working-class bars near port and industrial zones

### Churches / Religious Centers (TE) - 3
- **Various Christian Churches** - Catholic, Anglican, United Church, etc.
- **Sikh Temples (Gurdwaras)** - In South Vancouver and suburbs

- **Mosques** - Growing Muslim community centers

### Catacombs / Underground

## Special Features

- **Expo 86 Preparations** - Construction and development for world's fair
- **Skytrain Construction** - Beginning of rapid transit system
- **Chinatown** - One of largest in North America, cultural hub
- **Gastown** - Historic core, mix of shops, bars, and seedier elements
- **Port Activity** - Major shipping hub connecting Asia to North America
- **Drug Trade** - Heroin, cocaine flowing through port
- **Organized Crime** - Hells Angels, triads, Colombian cartels, Yakuza presence
