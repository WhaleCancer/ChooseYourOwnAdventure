# Prince George (1985)

**Settlement Type:** Town  
**Population:** ~67,000 (1985)  
**Origin:** Founded as Fort George trading post, grew as logging and pulp mill center.  
**Reason for Placement:** Confluence of Fraser and Nechako rivers, access to vast timber resources, central location for northern BC.  
**Current Occupation:** Logging industry workers, pulp mill employees, railway workers, government workers, First Nations communities.

## Special Features

- **Pulp and Paper Industry** - Major employer, mills visible from town
- **Logging Industry** - Central hub for northern BC logging
- **Railway Hub** - CN Rail classification yard, important for freight
- **Northern Gateway** - Access point to remote northern communities
- **Harsh Winters** - Cold, snow, isolation
- **Resource-Based Economy** - Dependent on forestry and mining
- **First Nations Presence** - Significant Indigenous population
- **Blue-Collar Character** - Working-class, industrial town atmosphere
- **Distance from Major Centers** - Isolated, self-sufficient community
