# Kelowna (1985)

**Settlement Type:** Town  
**Population:** ~61,000 (1985)  
**Origin:** Founded as a fruit-growing and agricultural center, grew as resort destination.  
**Reason for Placement:** Okanagan Lake waterfront, fertile valley for orchards, mild climate, scenic location.  
**Current Occupation:** Retirees, tourism industry workers, orchard/farm workers, small business owners, some light industry.

## Location Types

### City Gates / Highway Entrances (CG) - 1-2 on 1d6
- **Highway 97 North** - Entrance from Vernon, rest of Okanagan
- **Highway 97 South** - Entrance from Penticton, Osoyoos, border
- **Highway 33 East** - Entrance from Big White, rural areas
- **Okanagan Lake Bridge** - Connection to West Kelowna (not built until 2008, but ferry service exists)

### Streets (ST) - 1
- **Bernard Avenue** - Main downtown street, shops, restaurants
- **Harvey Avenue (Highway 97)** - Main commercial strip
- **Pandosy Street** - Mixed commercial/residential
- **Various residential areas** - Spread along lake and hillsides

## Special Features

- **Okanagan Lake** - Central feature, tourism, recreation
- **Orchards and Wineries** - Fruit industry, emerging wine industry
- **Retirement Destination** - Many retirees, mild climate
- **Tourism** - Summer resort destination, beaches, water sports
- **Mild Climate** - Hot summers, mild winters (compared to rest of Canada)
- **Scenic Location** - Mountains, lake, valley setting
- **Growing Community** - Rapid growth in 1980s
- **Recreational Focus** - Boating, skiing, outdoor activities
- **Agricultural Heritage** - Fruit packing, processing
