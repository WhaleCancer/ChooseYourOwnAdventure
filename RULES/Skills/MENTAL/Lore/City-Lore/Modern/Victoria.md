# Victoria (1985)

**Settlement Type:** City (smaller than Vancouver)  
**Population:** ~68,000 (1985)  
**Origin:** Founded as Hudson's Bay Company trading post (Fort Victoria), became provincial capital.  
**Reason for Placement:** Excellent natural harbor, strategic location for Pacific trade, proximity to naval base.  
**Current Occupation:** Government workers, retirees, tourism industry, naval personnel, university students.

## Special Features

- **Provincial Capital** - Government center, many government workers
- **Tourist Destination** - British character, tea rooms, gardens
- **Naval Base** - CFB Esquimalt nearby, naval personnel in city
- **Retiree Population** - Many retirees drawn by mild climate
- **Inner Harbour** - Iconic waterfront, tourist area, float planes
- **Mild Climate** - Warmer than rest of Canada, Mediterranean-like
- **Historic Architecture** - Many heritage buildings
- **Smaller Criminal Presence** - Less organized crime than Vancouver, but some drug trade
