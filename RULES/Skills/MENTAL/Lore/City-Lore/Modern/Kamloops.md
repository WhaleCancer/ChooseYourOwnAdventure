# Kamloops (1985)

**Settlement Type:** Town  
**Population:** ~62,000 (1985)  
**Origin:** Founded as a Hudson's Bay Company trading post, grew as railway junction and ranching/agricultural center.  
**Reason for Placement:** Confluence of North and South Thompson rivers, railway junction (CP Rail and CN Rail), access to Interior ranching country.  
**Current Occupation:** Railway workers, ranchers, government workers, forestry workers, university students.

## Special Features

- **Railway Hub** - Major CP Rail and CN Rail junction
- **Ranching Center** - Surrounded by cattle ranches
- **Semi-Arid Climate** - Hot dry summers, cold winters
- **Interior Location** - Gateway to BC Interior
- **Government Services** - Regional center for provincial services
- **CP Rail Operations** - Major railway maintenance and operations
- **Thompson Rivers** - Meeting of North and South Thompson
- **Hillside Development** - Built on terraces above river valley
- **Resource-Based Economy** - Forestry, ranching, some mining
