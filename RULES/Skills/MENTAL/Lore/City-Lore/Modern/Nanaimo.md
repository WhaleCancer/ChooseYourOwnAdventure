# Nanaimo (1985)

**Settlement Type:** Town  
**Population:** ~47,000 (1985)  
**Origin:** Founded as coal mining town (Hudson's Bay Company), transitioned to forestry and port activities.  
**Reason for Placement:** Deep-water harbor, coal deposits (mostly exhausted), access to Vancouver Island interior forests.  
**Current Occupation:** Forestry workers, port workers, government workers, retirees, some mining heritage.

## Special Features

- **Coal Mining Heritage** - Historic coal mining town, some operations may still exist
- **Deep-Water Port** - Major port for Vancouver Island
- **Ferry Gateway** - Main connection to Vancouver/Mainland
- **Forestry Industry** - Logging, pulp mills
- **Harbor City** - Built around protected harbor
- **Island Character** - Different pace than mainland
- **Maritime Atmosphere** - Fishing, shipping, ferry traffic
- **Access to Gulf Islands** - Gateway to nearby islands
- **Mild Climate** - Warmer than mainland interior
