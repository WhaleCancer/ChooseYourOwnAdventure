The Browning High Power (also known as the Hi-Power or P35) was manufactured in Canada by John Inglis and Company during World War II. The Canadian-produced version was used by Canadian and British forces, and production continued post-war. In British Columbia, the Browning High Power would be encountered with Canadian military personnel, RCMP officers, and civilians who own this popular and reliable 9mm pistol. The High Power's primary advantage is its 13-round magazine capacity, significantly higher than most contemporary pistols and revolvers, allowing for sustained fire without frequent reloading. The pistol also features excellent ergonomics and a reputation for reliability. It was one of the most widely used military pistols of the 20th century and remains popular with collectors and shooters.
