# Political Intrigue

These villains are a varied group, including corrupt officials, spies, manipulators, or talented individuals with charisma and connections. They are referred to as 'the professionals' who rely on acquired skills rather than brute strength or advanced technology.

## Table 9.2.4A Political Intrigue Type

Roll 1d6 to determine the specific type of political intrigue villain:

| Roll 1d6 | Type | Examples |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Corrupt Official | Admirals who break regulations, politicians who abuse power, officials who serve their own interests |
| 2 | Spy/Intelligence Agent | Section 31 agents, Tal Shiar operatives, undercover agents, those who manipulate from the shadows |
| 3 | Criminal/Smuggler | Smugglers, black market dealers, those who operate outside the law, organized crime leaders |
| 4 | Diplomatic Manipulator | Ambassadors who incite conflict, negotiators who serve hidden agendas, those who use diplomacy as a weapon |
| 5 | Corporate Executive | Business leaders who exploit resources, those who put profit over ethics, corporate spies |
| 6 | Social Engineer | Charismatic leaders who manipulate groups, cult leaders with political goals, those who use influence to control others |

## Characteristics and Skills

These villains focus on using their special skills to achieve their aims and are generally not afraid of combat, but prefer to avoid it. They have SKILL = 4+1d3, STAMINA = 10+1d6, MAGIC = 0. They have 2 points in Stealth special skills, 2 points in Armour (if they wear any), one type of hand weapon (phasers, disruptors, etc.), and 2 points in two out of the following skills: Bargain, Con, Etiquette, Law, or Leadership.
