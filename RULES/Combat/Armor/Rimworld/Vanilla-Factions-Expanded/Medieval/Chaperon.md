## Description

A stylish and versatile hood with a wrapped tail, favored by diplomats and merchants. It lends an air of authority, subtly improving negotiation skills.

Based on chaperons worn by merchants, diplomats, and middle-class professionals in medieval Europe, particularly during the 14th and 15th centuries. The chaperon was a versatile hood with a long tail (liripipe) that could be wrapped in various styles. This fashionable headdress was favored by merchants, scholars, and those who needed to project authority and respectability. The chaperon's elegant appearance and association with successful merchants and diplomats made it a symbol of social status and professional competence.
