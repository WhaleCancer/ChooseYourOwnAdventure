# VPD Sergeant

**Type:** Vancouver Police Department supervisor / Minion  
**Era:** 1985

## Overview

**VPD Sergeants** are supervisory officers who lead patrol units, coordinate operations, and make tactical decisions. These officers have risen through the ranks through experience and leadership ability, serving as the bridge between patrol officers and command staff. They're experienced, tactical, and responsible for directing police operations in the field.

## Statistics

**SKILL:** 8  
**STAMINA:** 10  
**HABITAT:** Police stations, patrol routes, and operational command  
**NUMBER ENCOUNTERED:** 1-2 (1d2)  
**TYPE:** Human  
**REACTION:** Unfriendly-Hostile (depending on circumstances)  
**INTELLIGENCE:** Average-High

## Characteristics

VPD Sergeants are:

- **Experienced** - Years of service and leadership experience
- **Tactical** - Skilled at coordinating operations and making decisions
- **Authoritative** - Command respect and can direct other officers
- **Dedicated** - Committed to protecting the public and enforcing the law
- **Strategic** - Think beyond immediate situations
- **Responsible** - Accountable for their units and operations

## Equipment

VPD Sergeants typically carry:

- **Handguns** - [Service revolvers](../../Weapons/Canada 198X/_Ranged/Handguns/Revolvers/Service Revolver.md) (Smith & Wesson Model 10, .38 Special) or service pistols
- **Shotguns** - [Pump-action shotguns](../../Weapons/Canada 198X/_Ranged/Shotguns/Pump Shotgun.md) (Remington 870) in patrol vehicles
- **Less-lethal** - Batons, handcuffs, and other restraint equipment
- **Radio** - For communication and coordination
- **Uniform** - Standard police uniform with sergeant's insignia
- **Body armor** - May wear soft body armor for protection

## Abilities

### Leadership

VPD Sergeants can command other officers. When leading other police officers, the sergeant grants +1 to SKILL to all police officers in the encounter for coordination and combat actions.

### Tactical Command

VPD Sergeants are skilled at coordinating operations. They gain +1 to SKILL for tactical decisions, coordinating units, and directing police operations.

### Experience

VPD Sergeants have extensive experience. They gain +1 to SKILL for awareness tests and may act first in surprise situations. They can read situations and respond appropriately.

### Authority

VPD Sergeants have significant authority. They can make tactical decisions, direct operations, and coordinate multiple units. Opponents may be intimidated by their authority and command presence.

### Backup Coordination

VPD Sergeants can coordinate backup and resources. They can call for additional units, specialized teams, or resources, potentially bringing significant police response within 1d4+1 rounds.

## In Play

VPD Sergeants serve as:

- **Command elements** - Leading and coordinating police operations
- **Tactical threats** - More experienced and strategic than patrol officers
- **Information sources** - Knowledge of police operations and procedures
- **Potential allies** - May assist legitimate investigations
- **Obstacles** - May coordinate police response against player characters
- **Story elements** - May be involved in larger investigations or operations

### Game Considerations

- Sergeants are experienced leaders, not just combatants
- They will coordinate operations and direct other officers
- Their authority makes them more dangerous than individual officers
- They may be more willing to negotiate than lower-ranking officers
- They often appear with other police officers as a command element
- Their presence indicates serious police response and coordination
