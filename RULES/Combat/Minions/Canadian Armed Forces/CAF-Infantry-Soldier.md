# CAF Infantry Soldier

**Type:** Canadian Armed Forces infantry soldier / Minion  
**Era:** 1985

## Overview

**CAF Infantry Soldiers** are the standard soldiers of the Canadian Armed Forces, trained in infantry tactics, marksmanship, and military operations. In 1985, the Canadian military is transitioning from the C1A1 to the new C7 assault rifle, with both weapons still in service. These soldiers are professional, disciplined, and well-trained for military operations.

## Statistics

**SKILL:** 8  
**STAMINA:** 10  
**HABITAT:** Military bases, training areas, and operational deployments  
**NUMBER ENCOUNTERED:** 3-12 (2d6)  
**TYPE:** Human  
**REACTION:** Hostile-Unfriendly  
**INTELLIGENCE:** Average

## Characteristics

CAF Infantry Soldiers are:

- **Trained** - Professional soldiers with military training
- **Disciplined** - Follow orders and maintain unit cohesion
- **Experienced** - Many have operational experience
- **Well-equipped** - Access to military weapons and equipment
- **Loyal** - Committed to their unit and mission
- **Professional** - Trained to handle combat situations

## Abilities

### Military Training

CAF Infantry Soldiers are trained in military tactics and marksmanship. They gain +1 to SKILL when fighting in groups of three or more, representing their ability to coordinate fire and maintain unit cohesion.

### Squad Tactics

CAF Infantry Soldiers are trained in squad tactics. When operating as part of a squad, they gain +1 to SKILL for coordination, movement, and combat actions.

### Suppressive Fire

CAF Infantry Soldiers are trained in suppressive fire tactics. When providing covering fire, they can force opponents to take cover, reducing the opponent's SKILL by -1 for one round.

### Bayonet Charge

When equipped with a bayonet, infantry soldiers can perform aggressive melee attacks. If they move into melee range and attack in the same round, they gain +1 to damage on the first melee attack.

## In Play

CAF Infantry Soldiers serve as:

- **Military encounters** - Professional soldiers in combat situations
- **Tactical threats** - Well-trained and coordinated
- **Information sources** - May have knowledge of military operations
- **Potential allies** - May assist legitimate operations
- **Obstacles** - May interfere with illegal activities or investigations

### Game Considerations

- Infantry Soldiers are professional military, not criminals
- They will follow orders and maintain unit cohesion
- Their training makes them effective in combat
- They often appear in squads or larger units
- Their presence indicates active military operations
- They may be more willing to negotiate than specialized units
