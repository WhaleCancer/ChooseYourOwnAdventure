## Description

A classic western-style revolver used by frontier settlers. Reliable handgun with good damage and moderate range. Single-action revolver typical of the frontier era. Iconic weapon of the American West, favored by settlers and lawmen.

Based on the Colt Single Action Army (introduced 1873), the legendary "gun that won the West." Chambered in .45 Colt, this single-action revolver requires manually cocking the hammer for each shot. The classic Western revolver design remains iconic and reliable, still encountered in rural areas of British Columbia where these historical weapons may be found in collections, surplus stores, or in the hands of enthusiasts who appreciate the frontier aesthetic and reliable design.
