## Description

A heavy machine gun designed for sustained automatic fire. Heavy and unwieldy, but delivers tremendous damage thanks to armor-piercing ammunition. Belt-fed ammunition ensures continuous fire as long as the person operating it can manage the weapon. These belt-fed machine guns fire full-power rifle ammunition (.30 caliber) or heavy-caliber rounds (.50 BMG), providing devastating firepower at extended ranges. Water-cooled variants offer sustained fire capability, while air-cooled versions are lighter but require more frequent barrel changes. These weapons are typically mounted on tripods, vehicles, or defensive positions due to their weight and recoil.

The most commonly encountered heavy machine guns are the M2 Browning .50 Caliber (1933), FN MAG/M240 (1958), and C6 GPMG (Canadian designation for FN MAG).
