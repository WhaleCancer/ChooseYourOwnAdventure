# 1985 Style Tribes – Clothing Reference

These entries describe visual “style tribes” in 1985 British Columbia.  
Use them as shorthand tags for clothing/equipment choices and as seed text for AI art prompts.

---

## 1. Mainstream “Mall” Girls & Boys

**Type:** Baseline mid‑80s teen and young adult fashion  
**Era/Region:** 1985, suburban and urban British Columbia  

**Look (visual prompt):** Big feathered or permed hair, pastel or primary‑color sweaters, high‑waisted straight‑leg jeans, slouch socks, white sneakers or canvas runners; for girls, oversized sweatshirts, pastel cardigans, simple hoop earrings, glossy lipstick; for boys, logo T‑shirts, varsity or jean jackets, baseball caps, basic leather or nylon jackets.  
**Where/Context:** Shopping malls, movie theatres, arcades, fast‑food counters, community colleges, bus stops after school.  
**Use in Game:** Default background look—when an NPC isn’t coded as any other tribe, they look like this; ideal for extras, bystanders, and “normal” friends or siblings of more extreme characters.

---

## 2. Yuppies & Office Girls

**Type:** Corporate, white‑collar, and aspirational professional fashion  
**Era/Region:** 1985, downtown Vancouver and business districts  

**Look (women – visual prompt):** Sharp shoulder‑padded blazers in navy, grey, or jewel tones; silk or polyester blouses with floppy bow ties; knee‑length pencil or A‑line skirts; sheer hose; closed‑toe pumps; structured leather handbags; discreet gold jewelry; hair in careful perms or blow‑outs.  
**Look (men – visual prompt):** Power suits with wide, padded shoulders; white or pale shirts; bold “power ties”; suspenders; polished dress shoes; leather briefcases; neatly parted or slicked hair, big glasses, conservative watches.  
**Where/Context:** Law firms, banks, brokerage offices, luxury hotels, Robson Street boutiques, conference rooms, upscale restaurants after work.  
**Use in Game:** Visual contrast with strip bars, SROs, and dive bars; natural clientele for escort agencies, hostess bars, high‑end sex workers; good for targets in white‑collar crime, leverage, or blackmail plots.

---

## 3. Preppy / Campus Clean‑Cut

**Type:** Upper‑middle‑class student and private‑school style  
**Era/Region:** 1985, UBC, SFU, UVic, private schools, affluent neighborhoods  

**Look (visual prompt):** Polo shirts with popped collars, rugby shirts with bold stripes, cable‑knit sweaters casually draped over shoulders, chinos or pressed khakis, clean jeans, penny loafers or boat shoes, sailing or windbreaker jackets, college hoodies, pastel and “nautical” color palettes; neat haircuts, minimal jewelry.  
**Where/Context:** University quads, student unions, campus pubs, yacht clubs, tennis courts, prep‑school grounds, family driveways with nice cars.  
**Use in Game:** Rich kids, student politicians, “nice” dates for working girls, easy marks for hustlers and scammers; useful contrast when dropped into rougher bars, strip clubs, or SRO corridors.

---

## 4. Glam‑Metal & Hard‑Rock Kids

**Type:** Hair‑metal and bar‑rock subculture  
**Era/Region:** 1985, club districts, strip bars, high‑school parking lots  

**Look (women – visual prompt):** Big teased hair with hairspray and blonde streaks, dramatic eyeliner, bright lipstick; leather or PVC miniskirts, fishnet stockings, ripped jeans, band T‑shirts cut into crop tops, studded belts, fingerless gloves; stilettos or cowboy boots, lots of bracelets and dangly earrings.  
**Look (men – visual prompt):** Long shaggy or feathered hair, bandanas tied around head or wrist, ripped or acid‑washed jeans, sleeveless denim or leather vests, concert shirts (Motley Crüe, Van Halen, etc.), studded belts, cowboy or engineer boots.  
**Where/Context:** Strip‑club neighborhoods, biker bars, high‑school parties, rock venues, alleyways behind clubs.  
**Use in Game:** Overlaps heavily with strip‑club and biker scenes; natural source of young strippers, dancers, bar staff, and streetwalkers; loud, visible NPCs who attract trouble and color every nightlife location.

---

## 5. Punk & Hardcore

**Type:** Punk, street‑level counterculture, and DIY activist fashion  
**Era/Region:** 1985, downtown Vancouver scenes, smaller echoes in Victoria  

**Look (visual prompt):** Leather or denim jackets covered in band logos and political patches, spikes and safety pins, plaid or tartan pants, torn jeans, studded belts and collars, band T‑shirts, mohawks or shaved sides, brightly dyed hair, black eyeliner; heavy boots like Doc Martens or army surplus combat boots.  
**Where/Context:** All‑ages shows, grimy live‑music clubs, record shops, cheap rentals near downtown, squats, back alleys around venues.  
**Use in Game:** Street kids, activists, troublemakers, survival‑level sex workers, characters clashing with cops and bikers; good for contacts who know the underground, local gossip, and where things are about to explode.

---

## 6. Skinheads (Various Stripes)

**Type:** Skinhead subculture, from racist gangs to apolitical to anti‑racist SHARPs  
**Era/Region:** 1985, overlapping with punk/hardcore scenes  

**Look (visual prompt):** Shaved or very close‑cropped hair, bomber or MA‑1 jackets in olive, black, or burgundy; braces (suspenders) over solid or striped shirts; rolled‑up straight‑leg jeans showing big boots; Doc Martens or steel‑toe work boots; flight badges, patches, and occasional political insignia.  
**Where/Context:** Back alleys near clubs, soccer fields, cheap bars, certain housing estates, street corners in contested neighborhoods.  
**Use in Game:** Ready‑made heavies or street enforcers; can be portrayed as racist gangs harassing immigrants, apolitical toughs for hire, or anti‑racist muscle protecting certain blocks, depending on tone and campaign darkness.

---

## 7. New Wave / Synth‑Pop / “Fashion Kids”

**Type:** Art‑school, college‑radio, and club‑oriented fashion  
**Era/Region:** 1985, clubs, campuses, art schools, alternative radio scenes  

**Look (visual prompt):** Asymmetrical haircuts, dyed or streaked hair, sharp eyeliner and bright make‑up; patterned blouses, oversized jackets, peg‑leg or tapered trousers, skinny ties, ankle boots or sleek heels; bold colors, geometric prints, layered jewelry; overall more polished and arty than punk.  
**Where/Context:** Trendy clubs, student art shows, college radio stations, photography studios, fashion boutiques, late‑night diners near venues.  
**Use in Game:** Art‑school part‑time sex workers, club kids, quirky NPCs who work as photographers, stylists, DJs; soft‑drug scene, people who drift between music venues, galleries, and after‑hours parties.

---

## 8. Goth / Deathrock

**Type:** Dark club, post‑punk, and macabre fashion  
**Era/Region:** 1985, niche but visible in city clubs  

**Look (visual prompt):** Black everything—long coats, lace blouses, layered skirts, ripped tights; big hair sprayed up or back‑combed, heavy black eyeliner and dark lipstick; crucifixes, ankhs, rosaries, silver jewelry; second‑hand Victorian or funeral‑parlor touches, fishnets, pointy boots.  
**Where/Context:** Late‑night clubs, gloomy cafes, occult or used bookstores, cemetery hangouts, back corners of record shops.  
**Use in Game:** Late‑night club patrons, spooky bookstore clerks, occult‑adjacent hooks, clients for more theatrical sex workers (peep shows, fetish rooms), people who feel at home around strange phenomena.

---

## 9. Bikers & Biker “Old Ladies”

**Type:** Outlaw biker gang style and close associates  
**Era/Region:** 1985, outskirts, small towns, outlaw bars, certain strip joints  

**Look (women – visual prompt):** Tight jeans or leather pants, leather vests or jackets, Harley or band T‑shirts, big hair with bandanas or scarves, heavy boots or high heels, bold make‑up, tattoos or patches showing club allegiance.  
**Look (men – visual prompt):** Leather cuts/vests with club patches, worn denim, plaid flannels, chain wallets, engineer or motorcycle boots, bandanas, beards or big mustaches, sometimes aviator sunglasses.  
**Where/Context:** Biker bars, roadhouses, clubhouses, highway gas stops, parking lots outside strip clubs.  
**Use in Game:** Crime syndicate foot soldiers and leadership; protection for parlours, clubs, and drug houses; serious danger for street‑level characters who cross them or wander onto their turf.

---

## 10. Metalheads & Thrash Kids

**Type:** Heavy metal and early thrash subculture  
**Era/Region:** 1985, mill towns, high schools, parking lots, cheap venues  

**Look (visual prompt):** Band shirts (Iron Maiden, Metallica, Slayer, Anthrax, etc.), faded or black denim jackets covered in patches, tight black jeans, studded belts and wristbands, white high‑top sneakers or worn boots; long hair on guys, bandanas, denim vests over hoodies.  
**Where/Context:** Mill‑town bars, suburban basements, school parking lots, cheap rock venues, record shops.  
**Use in Game:** Blue‑collar teens who grow into loggers, bikers, bouncers, or small‑time dealers; parking‑lot crowds, head‑banging extras at shows, easily recruited muscle for local gangs or smugglers.

---

## 11. B‑Boy / Early Hip‑Hop / Breakdance

**Type:** Early hip‑hop, street‑dance, and block‑party fashion  
**Era/Region:** 1985, especially visible in parts of Vancouver  

**Look (visual prompt):** Matching or mix‑and‑match track suits, windbreakers, shell‑toe Adidas or Puma with fat laces, bucket hats or snapbacks, large logo or graphic tees, nylon jackets, boom boxes on shoulders, fingerless gloves, sometimes bandanas; girls in oversized jackets, leggings, big earrings.  
**Where/Context:** Street corners, bus depots, mall courtyards, schoolyards, arcades, downtown sidewalks where kids practice breaking.  
**Use in Game:** Street‑corner dancers, small‑time hustlers and petty thieves with style, graffiti crews, kids who know the alleys and bus routes better than adults, especially in and around transit hubs and malls.

---

## 12. Skate / Surf / Snow Kids

**Type:** Skateboard, surf, and early snowboard/ski youth culture  
**Era/Region:** 1985, North Shore, Whistler, Victoria shorelines, urban skate spots  

**Look (visual prompt):** Vans or similar skate shoes, scuffed sneakers, baggy shorts or worn jeans, flannel overshirts, hoodies with surf or skate brand logos, beanies or trucker caps; for girls, oversized tees, cut‑off shorts, leggings, zip hoodies, sun‑bleached hair. Often carrying boards or gear.  
**Where/Context:** Skate parks, schoolyards, empty pools, parking garages, beach promenades, mountain towns, ski‑hill bars in off‑hours.  
**Use in Game:** Non‑criminal but fringe characters, weed dealers, couriers, message‑runners; ideal for NPCs who can move quickly through city or terrain, slipping past adults and cops.

---

## 13. “Hoser” / Blue‑Collar Canadian

**Type:** Working‑class Canadian stereotype, especially in resource and construction sectors  
**Era/Region:** 1985, logging towns, mills, construction sites, cheap taverns  

**Look (visual prompt):** “Canadian tuxedo” double denim—denim jacket and jeans; plaid flannel shirts over T‑shirts; work boots with worn leather; ball caps or wool toques; puffy winter coats in cold weather; women in quilted jackets, jeans, practical boots, simple hair and minimal make‑up.  
**Where/Context:** Mill‑town bars, resource‑town strip‑club audiences, construction sites, logging roads, union halls, roadside diners.  
**Use in Game:** Core patrons of working‑class bars and strip clubs; many johns and some working women off‑shift; solid, grounded NPCs who know local industry, gossip, and backroads.

---

## 14. Queer Club / Drag / Leather Bar Fashion

**Type:** Gay club, drag performance, and leather bar fashion  
**Era/Region:** 1985, specific pockets of Vancouver and a few other urban areas  

**Look (men – visual prompt):** Either “clone” look (tight jeans, white or grey T‑shirt, leather jacket, mustache) or sharp new‑wave/gay‑club chic with fitted shirts, vests, and stylish shoes; in leather bars, harnesses, tight leather pants, caps, boots; bold but controlled grooming.  
**Look (women – visual prompt):** Androgynous new‑wave (short hair, blazers, men’s shirts) or super‑feminine club looks (sequined dresses, big hair, dramatic make‑up); drag queens in sequins, feathers, elaborate wigs, towering heels, exaggerated make‑up.  
**Where/Context:** Gay bars, leather bars, after‑hours clubs, cruising areas, back rooms of certain nightclubs, queer‑run performance spaces.  
**Use in Game:** Access point to queer communities and trans and queer sex‑work scenes; characters who navigate hidden networks across Chinatown and downtown; stylish NPCs who know secrets, gossip, and who’s on the prowl or in danger.

---

## 15. Hong Kong / East Asian “Modern” Style

**Type:** Contemporary East Asian urban fashion, especially Hong Kong‑influenced  
**Era/Region:** 1985, Chinatown, downtown Vancouver, Richmond‑bound families  

**Look (adults – visual prompt):** Slightly sharper and more city‑polished than mainstream Canadians: neat blouses, slim trousers, tailored slacks, tidy perms, light make‑up; smart cardigans or blazers; brand‑name handbags; men in crisp shirts, light sweaters, leather or wool coats, understated watches.  
**Look (teens – visual prompt):** Pop‑star‑inspired outfits—layered skirts, patterned tights, ankle boots, bright sweaters, graphic tops, stylish jackets; boys in slick hairstyles, fitted jeans, casual jackets with subtle branding.  
**Where/Context:** Family‑run shops and restaurants, Chinatown streets, suburban homes, English‑language schools, hostess bars, malls frequented by immigrant families.  
**Use in Game:** Shopkeepers’ kids, hostesses, young women caught between “traditional” expectations and Western nightlife; visually sharp contrast to punks, bikers, and strippers; characters who bridge ethnic enclaves and broader city life.
