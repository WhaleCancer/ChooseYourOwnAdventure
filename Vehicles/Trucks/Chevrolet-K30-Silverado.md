# Chevrolet K20/K30 Silverado (1985 Heavy Duty 4x4)

**Type:** Heavy-duty pickup truck  
**Era:** 1985  
**Use:** Utility fleets, fire/rescue, power line and pipeline maintenance

## Overview

The classic "Chevy Squarebody" was the backbone of utility fleets, fire/rescue, power line and pipeline maintenance in BC. Powerful, boxy, and simple to fix in any small town, the K20/K30 can be found in industrial yellow or basic red.

## Modules

- **Utility Rack [1 Slot, 500Cr]:** Chainsaws, lights, shovels, cones, fire suppression kit.
- **Winch [1 Slot, 1,000Cr]:** Standard for line or forestry rescue.
- **First Aid Kit [1 Slot, 500Cr]:** For crew and bystanders; fire/EMS or utility trucks required this.

## Characteristics

The K20/K30 Silverado is known for:

- **Durability** - Extremely solid construction, designed for heavy-duty work
- **Reliability** - Simple to fix in any small town, making it ideal for remote operations
- **Versatility** - Suitable for a wide range of utility and emergency response tasks
- **Off-road capability** - 4x4 configuration handles rough terrain and remote locations
- **Common colors** - Industrial yellow or basic red for fleet identification

## Common Uses

- **Utility fleets** - Municipal and provincial maintenance operations
- **Fire/rescue** - Emergency response and fire suppression
- **Power line maintenance** - Electrical utility work in remote areas
- **Pipeline maintenance** - Oil and gas industry operations
- **Forestry** - Logging and forest management operations

## In Play

The K20/K30 Silverado serves as:

- **Utility vehicle** - Standard transportation for maintenance and emergency response
- **Off-road transport** - Access to remote locations for investigations
- **Equipment transport** - Hauling tools, equipment, and supplies for field work
- **Emergency response** - Fire, rescue, and emergency services operations
- **Fleet vehicle** - Common in government and utility fleets, providing legitimate cover

### Game Considerations

- The K20/K30 is extremely common in utility and government fleets, making it perfect for blending in
- The heavy-duty construction provides durability for rough terrain and remote operations
- The simple design makes it easy to maintain, important for remote investigations
- Common fleet colors (industrial yellow, basic red) help with identification and cover
- The vehicle's utility focus makes it natural for investigations involving infrastructure or remote locations
