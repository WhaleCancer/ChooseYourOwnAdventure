# CH-135 Twin Huey (Bell 212, 1985)

**Type:** Medium helicopter  
**Era:** 1985  
**Use:** Military, search and rescue, air ambulance, forestry, RCMP

## Overview

A mainstay medium helicopter of the Canadian Forces (and in occasional use by RCMP, forestry, and air ambulance agencies), the Bell 212 "Twin Huey" is the two-engined offspring of the legendary "Huey" line. Renowned for its stamina, ease of maintenance, and all-weather reliability, the Twin Huey flies medics, commandos, rangers, and supply teams far from paved roads.

## Typical Modules (Pick 4)

- **Passenger Bench [1 Slot, 500Cr]:** Up to 6 passengers; add second for full troop/crew load.
- **Rescue Hoist [1 Slot, 1,000Cr]:** For medevac/lift ops in mountain/forest/sea rescue.
- **Medical Module [1 Slot, 1,000Cr]:** Stretchers, trauma gear, oxygen; essential for air ambulance/SAR.
- **Cargo Net [1 Slot, 500Cr]:** Doors-off or external load, up to 1,200kg.
- **Long-Range Radio [1 Slot, 1,000Cr]:** Essential for search, command, wildfire, disaster operation.
- **Firefighting Kit [1 Slot, 1,000Cr]:** "Bambi bucket" or internal tanks for water bombing (forestry).
- **Survey/Field Desk [1 Slot, 500Cr]:** Map tables, weather/observation station.

## In Play

The CH-135 Twin Huey serves as:

- **Search and rescue** - Finding and rescuing people in remote areas
- **Air ambulance** - Medical evacuations and emergency response
- **Military transport** - Troop and cargo transportation
- **Forestry operations** - Firefighting and resource management
- **RCMP operations** - Law enforcement and surveillance

### Game Considerations

- The Twin Huey's versatility makes it suitable for many different missions
