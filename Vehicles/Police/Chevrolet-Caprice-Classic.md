# Chevrolet Caprice Classic (Police)

**Type:** Police cruiser  
**Era:** 1985  
**Use:** Standard police vehicle, pursuit package available

## Overview

The Chevy Caprice is perhaps the most iconic North American police cruiser of all time. By the mid-late 1980s, it was the default police car in much of Canada. Roomy, reliable, and available in both civil and high-output "9C1" pursuit packages, the Caprice became a symbol of policing from the Maritimes to Vancouver Island.

## Characteristics

The Caprice is a large, full-frame sedan with:

- **Reliability** - Known for durability and low maintenance
- **Roominess** - Comfortable interior with bench seating
- **Performance** - 9C1 package provides enhanced V8 power for pursuit
- **Visibility** - High profile makes it easily recognizable as a police vehicle

## Police Modifications

The 9C1 police package includes:

- Enhanced V8 engine for pursuit capability
- Police-grade glass and door armor inserts
- Reinforced bumpers
- Radio console for communications
- Trunk organizer for equipment storage
- Optional prisoner divider for transport
- Optional RCMP livery for federal police use

## Standard Modules

- **Radio Console [1 Slot, 1,000 CAD]:** Police or dispatch radio, often built in. Encrypted comms, long-range
- **Trunk Organizer [1 Slot, 500 CAD]:** Racks/cubbies for flares, accident markers, fire extinguisher, tool box, forms, evidence kits, etc.
- **Prisoner Divider [1 Slot, 500 CAD] (Optional):** A sturdy metal-and-polycarbonate screen installed between the front and rear seats, making prisoner escape difficult. Provides a -3 penalty to attempts to attack or interact with the front seat from the rear. May be forcibly bypassed by determined, strong criminals (SKILL test)
- **RCMP Livery [Optional]:** Official RCMP markings and colors for federal police use

### Optional Modules

- **Searchlight [1 Slot, 500 CAD]:** Large external lamp, can sweep by hand or power, blinds at night, good for rural/forest search & rescue
- **K9 Cage [1 Slot, 750 CAD]:** Reinforced crate for trained dog & handler transport; secures animal but can be removed for extra equipment or bench seat if no K9 assigned
- **Mobile Office [1 Slot, 500 CAD]:** Sliding tray, CB link, mini typewriter, forms, map drawers for command duties

## In Play

The Caprice serves as:

- **Standard police vehicle** - Most common police cruiser in 1985 British Columbia
- **Pursuit vehicle** - 9C1 package provides chase capability
- **Transport vehicle** - Room for multiple officers or prisoners
- **Symbol of authority** - Recognizable police presence

### Game Considerations

- The Caprice is the default police vehicle, making it common in law enforcement encounters
- The 9C1 package provides enhanced performance for chase sequences
- The large size and visibility make it less suitable for undercover operations
- Police modifications provide tactical advantages but also mark it as an official vehicle
