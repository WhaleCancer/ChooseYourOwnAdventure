## Description

Related to the Plesiosaurus, ELASMOSAURUS is

somewhat smaller in size, though most of that is

taken up by an even longer neck than its brethren.

Otherwise it is very similar in appearance, being a

large aquatic reptile with a broad, streamlined body

and four wide, flipper-like limbs. Being less robust,

Elasmosaurus tackles smaller prey such as schools

of fish and squid, as well as the occasional sea-bird,

but it is not averse to plucking an unlucky sailor
